Electronegativity. Electronegativity is a measure of the tendency of an atom to attract a shared pair of electrons towards itself. Electronegativity has no units. The most commonly used scale for its measurement is the Pauling scale. According to this scale, fluorine (the most electronegative element) is assigned a value of 4.0, and values range down to Caesium and Francium which are the least electronegative elements. Trends. (i) On moving from left to right in a period, there is a decrease in the size of the atoms. We will discuss electrical conductivity, melting point and boiling point in this section. 1. Electrical Conductivity. Electrical conductivity is the ability of a substance to conduct the electric current due to the presence of charged particles (free electrons, ions). Trends. Electrical conductivity of elements of period 3 increases as we move from sodium to aluminium and then decreases at the end of the period. Sodium, magnesium and aluminium are all good conductors of electricity. Conductivity increases as we move from sodium to aluminium due to increase in number of delocalized electrons. These delocalised electrons are free to move throughout the solid or the liquid metal. Silicon is a semiconductor. The remaining elements (P, S, Clâ‚‚, Ar) do not conduct electricity because they form simple molecular structure having no free electrons. 2. Melting and Boiling Points. The melting and boiling points tell us about the strength of forces present among atoms, ions and molecules within a substance. In the case of elements, the values of melting and boiling points are according to the bonding forces present in them. Trend. As we move from left to the right in a period, the melting and boiling points increase up to the middle i.e. group IV-A and then decrease from group V-A to noble gases (zero group elements.) Reasons. (1) The melting and boiling points are high up to the middle because these elements contain increasing number of binding electrons from I to III while silicon has giant covalent structure. (2) The melting and boiling points are low from P to Ar because these elements exist in the form of simple molecules and have weak intermolecular forces up to Argon. Quick Quiz. 1.
