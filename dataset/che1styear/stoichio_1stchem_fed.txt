
INTRODUCTION:
It is observed that an iron factory, a fireworks manufacturer, an engineer to design a new car and a chemist to manufacture a chemical plant is the amount of materials they use. That amount is a key factor in each specific area of work.

For example, a fireworks manufacturer is required to mix calculated amounts of chemicals. A wrong mixing amount could result in violent explosions.
An automobile engineer must know the relative amounts of fuel and oxygen needed for efficient combustion.
A chemist in a chemical plant adds calculated amounts of reactants to produce exact amount of products.
All these professionals practice their professions through the Knowledge of Stoichiometry (Greek, Stoicheon = element; metron = measurement). The study of relative quantities of reactants and products in a chemical reaction is called stoichiometry.

MOLE
A mole is a counting unit for atoms, molecules, ions etc. It is represented by the symbol mol. A mole is the amount of a substance that contains as many elementary particles (atoms, molecules, ions etc.) as there are atoms in exactly 12 grams of carbon-12. The mass of a mole of a substance expressed in grams is called molar mass. It is numerically equal to atomic mass and molecular mass of a substance.

Examples:
One mole of O (atom) = 16g
One mole of O2 (molecule) = 32 g
One mole of H2O (molecule) = 18 g
One mole of Na+ (ion) = 23 g
One mole of NaCl (Formula unit) = 58.5 g

AVOGADRO’S NUMBER
The number of atoms, ions or molecules present in one mole of a substance is called 'Avogadro’s Number'. Its numerical value is 6.023*10^23. One mole of any gas at STP occupies 22.414 dm^3 and contains 6.02 x 10^23 particles.

e.g., 2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O
This reaction can also be expressed in terms of Avogadro's number. The equation states that 2 x 6.02 x 10^23 molecules of hydrogen react with 6.02 x 10^23 molecules of oxygen to produce 2 x 6.02 x 10^23 molecules of water.

Mole Calculations:
The relationship between moles and Avogadro’s number in different substances is as under:

1 mole of O-atoms = 6.02 x 10^23 atoms
1 mole of O2 = 6.02 x 10^23 molecules
1 mole of H2O = 6.02 x 10^23 molecules
1 mole of NaCl = 6.02 x 10^23 formula units

In the case of ionic compounds, the explanation is somewhat different.
For example, NaCl(s) -> Na+(aq) + Cl-(aq)
It shows that 1 mole of NaCl when dissolves in water gives 1 mole of Na+ ions and 1 mole of Cl- ions. So According to Avogadro’s number we can say that when 6.02 x 10^23 formula units of NaCl are dissolved in water, they produce 6.02x10^23 Na+ and 6.02x10^23 Cl- ions.

Mole Ratios in Stoichiometric Calculations:
Mole ratio means the ratio of no. of moles of reactants taking part and the no. of moles of products formed. For example, combustion of propane:
C3H8 + 5O2 -> 3CO2 + 4H2O
The mole ratios of reactants and products: can be shown as:
C3H8 : O2 : CO2 : H2O = 1 : 5 : 3 : 4

Mass-Volume Relationship:
One mole of any gas at STP (standard temperature and pressure) occupies a volume of 22.414 dm^3. This volume is called Molar volume. With the help of this relationship we can convert the mass of a gas at STP into its volume and vice versa. Hence volume of gases is also related with their density at STP.
One mole of any gas at STP = 22.4 dm^3 = 6.02 x 10^23 molecules.
e.g., one mole of H2 gas at STP = 2g = 6.02 x 10^23 molecules.
e.g., one mole of NH3 gas at STP = 17g = 6.02 x 10^23 molecules.

PERCENTAGE COMPOSITION
The amounts of each element in a compound are expressed as the percentage composition. For example the percentage composition by mass in MgO is as follows,
To determine the percentage composition of a known compound:
Sum up the molar mass of compound.
Divide the mass of each element in one mole of the compound by the molar mass multiplied by 100.

Percentage of an element = (Mass of element in 1 mole of compound / Molar mass of compound) x 100

Example: Calculate the percentage composition of water.
Molar mass of H2O = 18 g mol-1
Mass of H in H2O = 2 g
Mass of O in H2O = 16 g
Percentage of H = (2 / 18) x 100 = 11.11%
Percentage of O = (16 / 18) x 100 = 88.89%

LIMITING AND EXCESS REACTANTS
The reactant that is consumed completely in a chemical reaction is called limiting reactant. It can also be defined as the reactant which produces the least number of moles of products in a chemical reaction. The reactant left un-used or un-reacted after completion of reaction is called reactant in excess or Non Limiting reactant.

Identification of a Limiting Reactant in a Reaction:
Limiting reactant can recognized by calculating the number of moles of products formed from the given amounts of the reactants, using a balanced chemical equation. The reactant which produces the least number of moles of products, is the limiting reactant.

For example, 10 moles of H2 and 7 moles of O2 were reacted to produce H2O. Which reactant is the limiting reactant? We can calculate as follows:
The reaction is: 2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O
According to balanced chemical equation:
2 moles of H2 = 2 moles of H2O
10 moles of H2 = (2/2) x 10 = 10 moles of H2O
1 mole of O2 = 2 moles of H2O
7 moles of O2 = 2 x 7 = 14 moles of H2O
Since H2 produces less moles of product, H2 is the limiting reactant.

Example:
200g of K2Cr2O7 were reacted with 200g conc. H2SO4. Calculate:
(a) Mass of atomic oxygen produced
(b) Mass of reactant left unreacted
The reaction is: K2Cr2O7 + 4H2SO4 -> K2SO4 + Cr2(SO4)3 + 4H2O + 3[O]
Mass of K2Cr2O7 = 200 g
Formula Mass of K2Cr2O7 = 294 g mol-1
No. of moles of K2Cr2O7 = 200 / 294 = 0.68 moles
Mass of H2SO4 = 200 g
Formula Mass of H2SO4 = 98 g mol-1
No. of moles of H2SO4 = 200 / 98 = 2.04 moles
According to balanced chemical equation:
1 mole of K2Cr2O7 = 3 moles of [O]
0.68 moles of K2Cr2O7 = 3 x 0.68 = 2.04 moles of [O]
4 moles of H2SO4 = 3 moles of [O]
2.04 moles of H2SO4 = (3/4) x 2.04 = 1.53 moles of [O]
Since H2SO4 produces lesser moles of product, H2SO4 is the limiting reactant.
Mass of oxygen = No. of moles of oxygen atom x atomic mass = 1.53 x 16 = 24.48 g
Since H2SO4 is the limiting reactant and K2Cr2O7 is the reactant in excess.
1 mole of K2Cr2O7 reacts with 4 moles of H2SO4
0.68 moles of K2Cr2O7 react with 4 x 0.68 = 2.72 moles of H2SO4
But we have only 2.04 moles of H2SO4.
So, moles of K2Cr2O7 that react = (1/4) x 2.04 = 0.51 moles
Moles of K2Cr2O7 left unreacted = 0.68 - 0.51 = 0.17 moles
Mass of K2Cr2O7 left = 0.17 x 294 = 49.98 g

Reactant Present in Excess in a Reaction:
When two reactants are reacted sometimes one of them is present in excess and the other is completely consumed. The concept of excess may be more clear by studying the following example.

Example:
1.87 moles of ammonium chloride were reacted with 1.35 moles of calcium hydroxide. How many Grams of calcium hydroxide are left unreacted in this reactions?
According to the reaction: Ca(OH)2 + 2NH4Cl -> CaCl2 + 2NH4OH
Calculate the no. of moles of Ca(OH)2 that react completely.
2 moles of NH4Cl = 1 mole of Ca(OH)2
1.87 moles of NH4Cl = (1/2) x 1.87 = 0.935 moles of Ca(OH)2
No. of moles of Ca(OH)2 consumed = 0.935 moles
No. of moles of Ca(OH)2 initially present = 1.35 moles
No. of moles of Ca(OH)2 unreacted = 1.35 - 0.935 = 0.415 moles
Molar mass of Ca(OH)2 = 74 g mol-1
Mass of Ca(OH)2 = No. of moles x Molar mass = 0.415 x 74 = 30.71 g
The excess amount of Ca(OH)2 which is left unreacted is 30.71 g.

THEORETICAL YIELD, ACTUAL YIELD AND PERCENT YIELD
The quantity of a product calculated from a balanced chemical equation is called theoretical yield of the reaction. The quantity of a product that is actually produced in a reaction is called the actual yield.

Percentage yield can be calculated as:
Percentage Yield = (Actual Yield / Theoretical Yield) x 100

Some reactions for which the actual yield is almost equal to the theoretical yield are called quantitative, i.e., they can be used in chemical analysis. On the other hand many reactions, particularly those involving organic compounds, the actual yield of a product is frequently less than theoretical yield and percent yield is less than 100 %.

Actual yield may be less than the theoretical yield due to following reasons:
Reactions are slow
Reactions are reversible
Side reactions produce by-products
Losses occur during purification due to filtration, distillation, and separation.

Example:
1.274 g of pure copper sulphate was electrolyzed. The mass of copper deposited was 0.392 g. Calculate the percentage yield.
Solution:
First convert grams of CuSO4 into no. of moles.
Molar mass of CuSO4 = 63.5 + 32 + 64 = 159.5 g mol-1
No. of moles of CuSO4 = 1.274 / 159.5 = 0.00799 moles
According to equation: CuSO4 -> Cu + S + 2O2
1 mole of CuSO4 = 1 mole of Cu
0.00799 moles of CuSO4 = 0.00799 moles of Cu
1 mole of Cu = 63.5 g
0.00799 moles of Cu = 63.5 x 0.00799 = 0.507 g of Cu.
Hence, Theoretical yield = 0.507 g
Actual yield = 0.392 g
So, % yield = (Actual yield / Theoretical yield) x 100 = (0.392 / 0.507) x 100 = 77.3%
